Gold Purity Guide

Understanding different types of gold and their purity levels

Gold Type Purity % Description
24K 99.9% Pure Gold: Typically used in gold coins, bars, and investment bullion. Soft and malleable, not suitable for jewelry.
22K 91.6% Traditional Jewelry: Most common purity for Indian gold jewelry. Good balance of purity and durability for ornaments and traditional jewelry.
21K 87.5% Premium Jewelry: High purity gold used in premium jewelry. Slightly more durable than 22K while maintaining high gold content.
18K 75.0% Modern Jewelry: Popular for modern jewelry, especially in international markets. More durable and suitable for daily wear.
14K 58.3% Fashion Jewelry: Commonly used in fashion jewelry. More affordable while still containing significant gold content.
10K 41.7% Entry Level: Minimum gold content to be considered "gold" in many countries. Very durable but lower gold value.
Quick Tip

Higher karat gold contains more pure gold but is softer. Lower karat gold is more durable but contains less pure gold. Our calculator automatically adjusts prices based on the exact purity of your gold items.
